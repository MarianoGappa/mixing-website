How it works

First of all

  • Are you sure you are looking for a Professional Mixer? Do you understand what a mixer does? If you are unsure, you might want to check the Wikipedia definition of Audio Mixing; it's very good and you'll learn something for sure!
  • Send me an email. I prepared a template for you. Please send me a rough demo of your song along with the email.

Before exporting your tracks

  • Great mixes come from great recordings; there's a limit to the magic I can do. Make sure you record great sounding tracks.
  • Please check your tracks; make sure nothing is missing and everything sounds right; otherwise we may find out too late to save money and time.
  • What makes your song great? If you don't know this already, I'll have to guess it, and if we disagree I could end up highlighting something you are not crazy about, etc. You must have some idea of what you want the end result to sound like.
  • Are the vocals in tune? Note that tuning the vocal tracks is editing, not mixing, and it's not included in the standard rate (I won't charge you for a few corrections, but complete tuning takes a lot of time and effort.)
  • Same goes for vocal comping; this is an editing task you want to do before you send your tracks.

Exporting your tracks

  • Export your individual audio tracks from the DAW (Digital Audio Workstation) used to record the songs. If you used a recording engineer or someone other than you to record the songs, or if you recorded the songs at a studio, ask them to send you your audio tracks.
  • The standard format for the individual audio tracks is mono WAV files, or interleaved (stereo) WAV files. It is important that they are exported at the best possible quality: 24 bit / 44kHz or 48kHz are decent quality standards.

Sending your tracks

  • The bulk of WAV files you need to send is likely to occupy as much as 500 MB or more; this doesn't fit on an email attachment.
  • Your best bet to send the files to me is by uploading them to some cloud storage system and sharing it with me by giving me a link to them. Two of the most popular free cloud storage services are Google Drive and Dropbox.
  • The standard format for the individual audio tracks is mono WAV files, or interleaved (stereo) WAV files. It is important that they are exported at the best possible quality: 24 bit / 44kHz or 48kHz are decent quality standards.
  • Please send me whatever you think will help me understand your vision for this song. Be smart: don't send me a 500 page book, I won't have time to read it and even if I did I would miss your highlights. Send me the lyrics, your notes, your ideas, maybe some reference songs? Help me love your project and I will mix it with more enthusiasm!

Initial check and 50% payment upfront

  • Once I receive your stuff and make sure everything is ok, I will give you instructions on how to make an upfront payment for 50% of the total cost described here.
  • After the payment clears out, I will start working on your mix. I might contact you if I feel like I need to run some decisions by you.

Review and final mix

  • When I finish a draft mix, I will send it to you in MP3 format so that you can review it and give me some feedback. You need to be happy with your final product. I'll do my best to understand your vision, but you need to be responsible and smart about the review process: extensive review processes will mean extra charge.
  • Once you're happy with your mix, I'll send you the final mix package upon clearing of the final 50% payment.
  • Finally, let's review the experience. Are you satisfied with the product? Was it a smooth process? Email me your review. This way I can learn how to work better, and I can make better mixes for you the next time. Can I quote you on this site? Can I showcase a sample of your mix?
